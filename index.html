Trump Signs Executive Order to Fast-Track Psychedelic Research

In April 2026, Trump signed an executive order accelerating research into psychedelic therapies for mental illness. The FDA has since issued priority review vouchers to three companies developing psilocybin and methylone treatments for depression and PTSD, and approved a phase 1 trial of noribogaine for alcohol use disorder. A $50 million federal initiative targets ibogaine for veteran mental health.

Psilocybin for Treatment-Resistant Depression: 12-Month Follow-up of Phase 2 RCT
New England Journal of Medicine · PubMed · Published Jan 2026
Psilocybin Phase 2
Population 233 adults with treatment-resistant MDD (≥2 failed antidepressants), ages 18–65
Intervention Single 25mg psilocybin session + 8hr therapist support; double-blind placebo-controlled
Key Finding 71% response rate at 12 weeks; 29% full remission at 6 months (vs 8% placebo). Sustained response correlated with mystical-experience score at session.
Status Published · Phase 3 now recruiting (NCT05984628) · FDA Breakthrough Therapy Designation held

MDMA-Assisted Therapy for PTSD: MAPP2 Phase 3 Global Trial — 12-Month Interim Analysis
MAPS · ClinicalTrials.gov · NCT05765032 · Active
MDMA Phase 3
Population 1,200 adults with severe PTSD (CAPS-5 ≥35) across 20 sites in US, EU, and Australia
Intervention 3 MDMA sessions (80–120mg) + manualized integrative psychotherapy protocol over 18 weeks
Key Finding 68% no longer meet PTSD criteria at 12-month interim; mean CAPS-5 reduction 24.4 pts (placebo: 9.1). Serious adverse events 2.1%; no abuse signals.
Status Active · Enrolling · Est. completion Dec 2026 · NDA filing targeted Q2 2027

Ketamine and Esketamine in Major Depression: Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis of 68 RCTs
Lancet Psychiatry · PubMed · Published Mar 2026
Ketamine Meta-analysis
Population 8,240 patients across 68 RCTs; unipolar and bipolar MDD; multiple administration routes
Intervention IV ketamine 0.5mg/kg over 40 min, or intranasal esketamine (Spravato); 1–6 sessions
Key Finding NNT 3.2 for acute response; effect size d=0.89; 52% maintain response at 4 weeks. Dissociation risk elevated in ketamine-naive patients.
Status Published · NICE review pending · FDA Spravato labeling update under consideration
